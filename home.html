What is Alzheimer's disease?

Alzheimer's is a type of dementia that causes problems with memory, thinking and behavior. Symptoms usually develop slowly and get worse over time, becoming severe enough to interfere with daily tasks.

Stages of Alzheimer's

Mild/Early Stage

Even if the person is still functioning independently, he/she may feel as if there are memory lapses, such as forgetting familiar words or location of different objects.

Moderate/Middle Stage

Middle stage is typically the longest stage and may last for many years. You may notice him/her confusing words around, getting frustrated, or acting in unexpected ways, such as refusing to bathe. Damage to nerve cells in the brain make it difficult to express thoughts and perform routine tasks.

Severe/Late Stage

In the final stage, individuals lose the ability to respond to their environment, to carry on a conversation, and eventually, control movement. They may still say words or phrases, but communicating pain becomes difficult. Significant personality changes may take place and they will need extensive help with daily activities.
